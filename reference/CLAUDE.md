## Campfire Product — The Three Pillars
Campfire is a **finance operating system** built AI-native from day one. Position as "finance OS" not "ERP."

### 1. Revenue Automation
ASC 606 revenue recognition, usage-based billing, deferred revenue, automated revenue schedules, contract modifications. Handles complex rev rec that breaks QuickBooks and frustrates NetSuite teams.

### 2. Core Accounting
Multi-entity consolidation with intercompany eliminations, multi-currency with triple-ledger FX (functional, reporting, transaction currencies), automated close management with rule engine and exception-based workflows. Replaces manual journal entries, FloQast, and spreadsheet-based close processes.

### 3. Ember AI (AI Copilot)
Trained on financial data — not a bolted-on chatbot. Capabilities: anomaly detection, natural language queries ("show me all revenue variances over $10K"), predictive close timelines, automated journal entry suggestions, intelligent transaction categorization.

### Key Value Props
- **Speed to value:** Weeks to implement, not months (in-house implementation team, white-glove onboarding, dedicated Slack channels)
- **$5M NetSuite Buyout Fund:** Cover remaining contract costs for prospects locked into NetSuite
- **Self-service reporting:** Finance teams build their own reports without IT/engineering
- **API-first integrations:** Plaid, Ramp, Brex, Rippling, Stripe, Mercury + custom via REST API (4-8 week timeline for custom builds)
- **SOC 1 & SOC 2 certified**
- **Transaction-level data migration** (not summary-level like competitors)

## Discovery Framework (from Scott Wiki — Beginner's Guide to Selling Modern ERP)
Three discovery pillars to cover:

### 1. Finance Operating Model
- What's your role and how does your team operate day-to-day?
- Walk me through your month-end close process. How long does it take?
- How many entities do you manage? Any intercompany transactions?
- What does your tech stack look like? (ERP, billing, banking, payroll, expense)
- How do you handle rev rec today? (ASC 606 compliance, manual vs automated)
- Any multi-currency needs? How do you handle FX?

### 2. Cost of Pain
- How many hours per month does your team spend on manual journal entries?
- What's the "spreadsheet tax" — how many spreadsheets supplement your ERP?
- When's the last time you caught an error that should have been automated?
- What does audit prep look like? How long does it take?
- If you could eliminate one manual process, what would it be?

### 3. Why Now / Timeline
- What's driving the evaluation right now?
- What happens if you don't make a change this quarter?
- Are you considering going public? (IPO readiness is a strong Campfire use case)
- Who else is involved in this decision? What does the approval process look like?
- What other solutions are you evaluating?

## Accounting Terminology Quick Reference
When selling to finance leaders, know these concepts (full guide in Scott Wiki — "Accounting Core Concepts"):
- **Rev rec (ASC 606):** Revenue recognition standard — 5-step model. Red flag: "We do rev rec in spreadsheets"
- **Month-end close:** Process of finalizing books. Campfire automates via rule engine. Red flag: "Our close takes 15+ days"
- **Multi-entity consolidation:** Combining financials across subsidiaries. Red flag: "We consolidate in Excel"
- **Intercompany eliminations:** Removing internal transactions from consolidated books. Pain point for growing companies
- **Chart of accounts:** Structure for categorizing transactions. Campfire supports multi-segment
- **Prepaids & accruals:** Timing-based entries. Campfire automates amortization schedules
- **Usage-based billing:** Complex billing tied to consumption. Campfire handles natively (ASC 606 compliant)
- **Bank reconciliation:** Matching bank feeds to GL. Campfire integrates with Plaid for automation
